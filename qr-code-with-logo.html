Why Branded QR Codes Get More Scans

Independent QR analytics studies (Bitly, QR Code Generator, Beaconstac) consistently report that QR codes with a centered logo or image receive 20–35% higher scan rates than plain codes when placed in the same context. Three reasons:

  • Trust signal. A QR code with your logo is verifiable at a glance. A plain code on a flyer or product looks like it could be anything — including a phishing redirect.
  • Brand recall. A scannable code that visually carries your brand reinforces brand identity on every print piece — flyers, packaging, signage, posters, conference handouts.
  • Visual contrast. A custom-colored, logo-centered QR code stands out against design layouts. Plain black squares look like raw machine output and get visually ignored.

For high-stakes placements — printed marketing collateral, real estate "For Sale" signs, restaurant menus, conference handouts, product packaging — the lift from branding is the difference between a code that gets scanned and a code that gets ignored.

URL, Text, or Wi-Fi: Pick the Right Type

Different contexts call for different QR code content types. Here's how to choose:

  • URL QR code. The default for almost any marketing use case. Anyone who scans the code is sent straight to your website, landing page, store, menu, or campaign URL. Best for flyers, posters, packaging, and signage.
  • Text QR code. Encodes a literal text message that displays in the scanner app. Useful for serial numbers, SKUs, license plates, ticket codes, or short notes that don't need to live on a website.
  • Wi-Fi QR code. Includes the SSID, password, and encryption type. When a guest scans, their phone prompts them to join the network — no typing the password. Perfect for cafes, hotels, co-working spaces, and Airbnbs.

How Logo Embedding Works (The Technical Bit)

QR codes are designed with built-in error correction. A QR code can be partially obscured, scratched, or covered and still scan correctly because every code carries redundant data. There are four error-correction levels: L (~7% recovery), M (~15%), Q (~25%), and H (~30%). Our generator uses Level H by default, which means up to 30% of the code area can be covered — including by your logo — without breaking scannability.

In practice, we cap logo coverage at ~20% of the code surface and place it dead-center, where the structural finder patterns (the three big squares in the corners) are not located. The result is a code that scans reliably from any modern phone camera while displaying your logo prominently.

Always test by scanning the downloaded QR code with at least two different phones before printing in volume. We provide a live preview at every step so you can verify scannability before paying.

Best Practices for Branded QR Codes

  • Use SVG or transparent-background PNG for your logo so it sits cleanly on the code background.
  • Keep logo coverage under 20%. Larger logos look prominent but reduce scan reliability in low-light or angled-camera conditions.
  • Maintain high contrast between code dots and background. Avoid dark-on-dark or light-on-light combinations. Black or near-black on white is most reliable.
  • Print at 300 DPI minimum. A QR code printed at 72 DPI on a glossy flyer will frequently fail to scan.
  • Leave a 4-module quiet zone around the code (white space). Most professional templates include this automatically — don't crop into it.
  • Test before mass-printing. Scan from 6, 12, and 24 inches away, on iOS and Android, in normal indoor lighting.
