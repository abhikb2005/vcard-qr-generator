Why People Look for Mini QR Alternatives

Mini QR is a free, open-source QR code generator built with Vue.js. It’s hosted on Vercel and has earned 1,900+ GitHub stars thanks to its beautiful design customization—custom colors, dot shapes, corner styles, frames, and logo uploads. It supports vCard as one of many data templates.

But professionals who need vCard QR codes for business cards run into some gaps:

  • General-purpose tool — Mini QR handles URLs, text, email, Wi-Fi, vCard, locations, and calendar events. vCard is just one template, not the primary focus.
  • No dedicated vCard form — You fill in raw vCard data fields rather than a guided form designed for contact information. No field validation or phone compatibility testing.
  • Batch export requires CSV setup — Mini QR’s batch feature imports CSV data strings, but it’s designed for generic QR codes, not specifically for vCard bulk generation with proper field mapping.
  • Design-first, function-second — Mini QR excels at making QR codes look beautiful. But for business cards, you need reliable vCard 3.0 formatting and cross-device contact saving—not just aesthetics.
  • Self-hosted or demo only — The main app runs on a Vercel demo. For production use, you’re expected to self-host with Docker or a web server—not ideal for non-technical users.

Mini QR is an excellent open-source project for developers and designers. For dedicated vCard contact sharing at scale, a purpose-built tool is simpler and more reliable.

When Mini QR Is the Better Choice

To be fair, Mini QR is the better tool in certain scenarios:
