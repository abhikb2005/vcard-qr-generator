Why People Look for QR Code Generator Alternatives

QR Code Generator (qr-code-generator.com) is a well-established platform backed by Bitly. It supports dozens of QR code types, offers enterprise-grade analytics, and integrates with the Bitly Connections Platform for link management. For marketing teams running large-scale campaigns, it’s a solid choice.

But many users—especially those who just need a vCard QR code for a business card—run into friction. Here’s why:

  • Expensive PRO plans — PRO starts at €8/month (∼$9.99 USD) billed annually. That’s €96/year just for 2 dynamic QR codes. If you only need a few vCard codes, a yearly subscription is hard to justify.
  • Free tier limitations — The free version creates static vCard QR codes, but downloads are limited to JPG only. PNG, SVG, and EPS formats require a PRO account. Logo overlays and scan tracking are also locked behind the paywall.
  • Account required for most features — While basic static codes work without signup, anything beyond that—custom logos, dynamic codes, bulk creation, analytics—requires creating an account and entering payment details.
  • Server-side data processing — Dynamic QR codes and PRO features process your contact data on their servers. For privacy-conscious users, uploading personal phone numbers and email addresses to a third-party cloud is a concern.
  • Feature overload — QR Code Generator supports URL, WiFi, PDF, SMS, Bitcoin, app store links, and more. If you only need vCard codes, the interface is unnecessarily complex.

None of this makes QR Code Generator a bad product—it’s one of the best in the market for enterprise use. But if your primary need is creating QR codes for business cards, a purpose-built free tool is the better fit.
