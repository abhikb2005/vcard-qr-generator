What Is Flowcode?

Flowcode is an enterprise-grade QR code platform founded in 2019 by Tim Armstrong, former CEO of AOL and President of Google Americas. The company positions itself as the “#1 trusted QR provider” and claims adoption by over 70% of Fortune 500 companies. Flowcode offers custom-designed QR codes (called Flowcodes), mobile-optimized landing pages (Flowpages), scan analytics, audience insights, retargeting tools, and integrations with CRMs like HubSpot, Salesforce, and Mailchimp.

For users who simply need a vCard QR code for their business card, Flowcode’s enterprise-focused platform is massive overkill. The free plan limits you to just 2 codes, and the Personal plan at $25/month is designed for marketing teams — not individuals who need a single contact QR code.

Why Look for a Flowcode Alternative?

  • Only 2 free codes — Flowcode’s Basic plan gives you just 2 Flowcodes total. If you need codes for yourself and a colleague, you’ve already hit the limit. Upgrading to the Personal plan costs $25/month for 50 codes.
  • Analytics cap on free plan — While scans are unlimited, the free plan only tracks 500 analytics scans. After that, you lose visibility into scan data. For vCard QR codes on business cards, analytics are often unnecessary anyway.
  • Enterprise-level pricing — The Growth plan starts at $250/month ($3,000/year). Enterprise contracts average around $8,500/year. These plans are built for marketing teams running multi-channel campaigns, not for creating a contact QR code.
  • Seat-based restrictions — The free plan allows 1 seat. The Personal plan allows 5. If your small team needs individual vCard QR codes, you either share an account or pay for a higher tier.
  • Server-dependent dynamic codes — All Flowcodes are dynamic, meaning every scan routes through Flowcode’s servers. This enables editing but also means your contact sharing depends on their infrastructure staying online.
  • Privacy implications — Flowcode tracks scan location, device type, operating system, and timing for every scan. Their platform also supports retargeting scanned audiences. For privacy-conscious networking, this level of tracking may not be acceptable.
