What Is ME-QR?

ME-QR (me-qr.com) is a freemium dynamic QR code platform that has grown to over 7 million users with 135 million QR codes created globally. The platform supports 40+ QR code types — from URLs and WiFi credentials to restaurant menus and social media links — and offers scan analytics, custom designs with 1,000+ frames, and a mobile app for both iOS and Android.

ME-QR’s free tier is generous in terms of volume: you can create up to 10,000 QR codes with unlimited scans and unlimited lifetime. The catch is that every free QR code displays Google Ads when scanned. This means anyone scanning your vCard QR code from a business card will see an advertisement before reaching your contact information. Paid plans (Lite and Premium) remove ads and extend analytics history from 1 year to 3 years. ME-QR also offers bulk QR creation, API integration, and a ticket platform (ME-Ticket) for event organizers. For someone who needs a simple, professional contact-sharing QR code on a business card, ME-QR’s feature set is far more than necessary — and the ads on free codes are a dealbreaker.

Why Look for an ME-QR Alternative?

  • Ads on free QR codes — Every QR code created on ME-QR’s free plan shows Google Ads when scanned. Handing someone your business card and having them see an ad before your contact info is unprofessional and undermines trust.
  • Not native vCard encoding — ME-QR lists “vCard” as a QR type, but the QR code creates a dynamic redirect through their servers to a hosted page. It does not encode vCard 3.0 data directly in the QR pattern, so scanning doesn’t trigger an instant contact-save prompt.
  • Server dependency — All ME-QR codes are dynamic, meaning they route through ME-QR’s servers. If the service goes down or your account is removed, your QR codes — including any on printed business cards — stop working.
  • Signup required — You need an account to create, manage, and edit QR codes. For a one-time contact QR code, this adds unnecessary friction.
  • Privacy concerns — ME-QR tracks every scan with analytics (location, device, time) and even sends email notifications per scan. Your contacts’ scan behavior routes through their servers. For privacy-conscious users, this is a significant issue.
