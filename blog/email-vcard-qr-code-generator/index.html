What Is an Email vCard QR Code?

A vCard QR code encodes your contact details—name, phone numbers, email addresses, company, job title, and website—into a small square image that any smartphone camera can read. When someone scans the code, their device prompts them to add you directly to their contacts app. Unlike a traditional signature block where recipients must copy-paste or retype your information, the QR code automates the entire process. Embedding this code in your email signature means every message you send carries a portable, error-free business card that works across all platforms and devices.

Best Size and Format for Email

Recommended dimensions: Keep your QR code between 100 and 150 pixels wide for email signatures. This size balances scanability with signature aesthetics—large enough for cameras to read reliably, compact enough not to dominate your sign-off.

File format: Always use PNG. It preserves sharp edges, supports transparency if you want the code to blend with colored backgrounds, and doesn't introduce compression artifacts that could interfere with scanning.

Alt text: Add descriptive alt text like "Scan to save my contact details" so recipients with images disabled understand what they're missing. This also improves accessibility for screen reader users.

Quiet zone: Ensure the QR code has adequate white space around it. Most generators include this automatically, but if you crop the image, preserve at least a one-module border.

Troubleshooting Email QR Codes

Images blocked by default: Many email clients hide images until the recipient clicks "Display images" or adds you to their contacts. Include clear alt text and consider adding a text fallback like "(Scan to save contact)" near the code.

QR code too small: If recipients report scanning difficulties, increase the image size to at least 120 pixels. Test in different email clients, as some compress images during rendering.

Broken or outdated information: If your phone number or email changes, you'll need to generate a new QR code and update your signature. Unlike dynamic QR codes, static vCard codes can't be edited after creation. For more tips, see our QR code business contact tips.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size should my email signature QR code be?

For email signatures, use a QR code between 100-150 pixels wide. This size is large enough to scan reliably while fitting neatly within standard signature layouts.

Will my QR code work if the email is forwarded?

Yes. Unlike clickable links that may break, an embedded QR code image travels with the email when forwarded, allowing new recipients to scan and save your contact details.

Why is my email QR code not displaying for some recipients?

Many email clients block images by default. Recipients need to click "Display images" or add you to their safe senders list. Always include alt text describing the QR code.

Should I use PNG or JPG for email signature QR codes?

PNG is the preferred format because it preserves sharp edges and supports transparency. JPG compression can blur the QR pattern and reduce scan reliability.

Can I add a vCard QR code to PDF documents?

Absolutely. Embed the QR code in proposals, invoices, contracts, and newsletters. Recipients can scan directly from their screen or a printed copy.

Is my contact data secure when generating a vCard QR code?

Our generator runs entirely in your browser. Your contact information never leaves your device or gets stored on any server.
