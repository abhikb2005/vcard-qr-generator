Download Formats Explained

PNG (Portable Network Graphics) is the most versatile format for vCard QR codes. It offers excellent quality for digital use, supports transparent backgrounds, and works on virtually every platform. Choose PNG for email signatures, website embedding, social media profiles, and digital documents.

SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics) uses mathematical formulas instead of pixels, allowing infinite scaling without quality loss. This makes SVG ideal for print materials where you might need to enlarge the QR code significantly. Professional printers often prefer SVG files for business cards, brochures, and large-format printing like banners or trade show displays.

For most users, PNG at high resolution handles 90% of use cases. Reserve SVG for professional print jobs where the printer specifically requests vector files or when you need the same QR code at multiple sizes.

Recommended Resolution Settings

Resolution requirements differ significantly between digital and print applications. For digital use (screens, websites, emails), 72-150 DPI works perfectly. A 200x200 pixel QR code displays clearly on most screens and loads quickly without consuming excessive bandwidth.

For print applications, minimum 300 DPI ensures crisp, scannable codes. Calculate your pixel requirements by multiplying the physical size (in inches) by the DPI. A 1-inch QR code on a business card needs 300x300 pixels minimum. For larger prints, scale proportionally—a 3-inch QR code requires 900x900 pixels at 300 DPI.

When in doubt, download at the highest resolution available. You can always reduce size later, but you cannot add quality to an already-downloaded low-resolution file. Check our printing guide for detailed print specifications.

Saving and Organizing Your QR Files

Adopt a consistent naming convention to keep your QR code files organized. A structure like lastname-firstname-vcard-YYYY-MM-v1.png includes all essential identifiers: your name, file purpose, creation date, and version number. This becomes invaluable when managing multiple QR codes or updating contact information.

Create a dedicated folder for QR assets within your documents or cloud storage. Include subfolders for different formats (PNG, SVG) and use cases (print, digital). Back up your QR code files to cloud storage like Google Drive, Dropbox, or iCloud to prevent loss if your device fails.

Maintain version control by incrementing the version number (v1, v2, v3) each time you generate an updated QR code with new contact details. Never delete older versions immediately—keep them archived until you've confirmed all printed materials and digital placements have been updated.

Using Your Downloaded QR Code

Print applications: Add your QR code to business cards, letterheads, brochures, name badges, and promotional materials. Maintain adequate quiet zone (white space) around the code—at least 4 modules wide—and ensure sufficient contrast between the code and background. Learn the fundamentals in our complete vCard guide.

Email signatures: Insert the QR code as an embedded image in your email signature. Keep the display size around 80-100 pixels for optimal visibility without dominating the signature block. Most email clients support PNG images directly.

Website integration: Upload the QR code to your contact page, about section, or footer. Add appropriate alt text like "Scan to save contact information" for accessibility. Consider adding a brief instruction near the code for users unfamiliar with QR scanning.

Social media: Share your QR code on LinkedIn profiles, Twitter headers, Instagram posts, or Facebook business pages. Some platforms compress images, so upload at higher resolution than needed to maintain scannability after compression.

Troubleshooting Download Issues

Download not starting: Check if your browser's popup blocker is preventing the download. Temporarily disable it or add our site to the allowed list. Also verify you have sufficient storage space on your device.

File appears corrupted: Clear your browser cache and try downloading again. If the issue persists, switch to a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Edge typically work best). Ensure your internet connection is stable during the download.

Wrong file size or format: Return to the generator and verify your format and resolution selections before clicking download. Some browsers may rename files during download—check your downloads folder for recently modified files if you can't locate the expected filename.

Frequently Asked Questions

What format should I download my vCard QR code in?

Use PNG for most digital applications like email signatures, social media, and websites. Choose SVG for print materials where you need to scale the QR code to any size without losing quality.

What resolution do I need for printing my QR code?

For professional print quality, export your QR code at 300 DPI minimum. For business cards, a 1-inch QR code at 300 DPI means 300x300 pixels. For larger prints like posters, scale up proportionally.

Why won't my QR code download?

Common causes include browser popup blockers, insufficient storage space, or slow internet connection. Try disabling popup blockers, clearing browser cache, or using a different browser like Chrome or Firefox.

Can I edit my QR code after downloading it?

The downloaded QR code image cannot be edited to change the contact information. You need to generate a new QR code with updated details and download a fresh copy.

How should I name my QR code files?

Use a consistent naming convention like "yourname-vcard-qr-2025-v1.png". Include your name, file type, date, and version number for easy organization and version tracking.

Is my downloaded QR code file secure?

Yes. Our generator processes everything in your browser, so your contact data never reaches our servers. The downloaded file is a standard image that contains only the encoded vCard data.
