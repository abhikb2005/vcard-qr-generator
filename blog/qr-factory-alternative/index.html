Why People Look for QR Factory Alternatives

There are actually two products called “QR Factory.” The first is a web platform at qr-factory.com, a London-based SaaS offering dynamic QR codes with analytics. The second is a macOS/iPadOS desktop app by Tunabelly Software. Both let you create QR codes, but both have limitations for simple vCard contact sharing.

Professionals searching for a vCard QR code solution run into these common issues:

  • Subscription pricing — The web platform starts at €15/month, with a €250 lifetime option. The desktop app costs $14.99/year or $29.99 one-time. Either way, you’re paying for a simple contact code.
  • QR code limits — Even on paid plans, the web platform caps you at 50 QR codes. That’s a problem for teams needing bulk vCard generation.
  • Account required — Both platforms require signup or purchase before you can generate anything. For a quick one-off task, that’s unnecessary friction.
  • Platform lock-in — The desktop app only runs on macOS and iPadOS. Windows, Linux, and Android users are left out entirely.
  • Cloud data storage — The web platform stores your contact data on their servers. Privacy-conscious users want browser-based generation with no uploads.
  • Generic QR tool — vCard is just one of many QR types QR Factory supports. The vCard workflow isn’t optimized the way a dedicated tool would be.

QR Factory serves businesses needing multi-purpose QR solutions with tracking. For dedicated vCard contact sharing, a free, purpose-built tool is the more efficient choice.

When QR Factory Is the Better Choice

To be fair, QR Factory is the better tool in specific scenarios:

  • You need dynamic QR codes with tracking — If you want editable destinations and scan analytics, QR Factory’s web platform offers that on paid plans.
  • You prefer a native desktop app — If you work exclusively on macOS and want an offline-capable app, Tunabelly’s QR Factory desktop app is well-designed.
  • You need multiple QR code types — If you create URL, WiFi, PDF, and vCard codes in one place, QR Factory’s variety is convenient.

If none of those apply—if you just need vCard QR codes for business cards and networking—you’re paying for features you won’t use.
