Why People Look for Kaywa QR Code Alternatives

Kaywa (qrcode.kaywa.com) has been around for years and claims over 2 million customers. It offers URL, contact, coupon, text, phone, and SMS QR codes, with paid plans that include dynamic codes, scan tracking, campaign analytics, and custom branding. For enterprise marketing teams, it’s a solid option.

But for users who just need a vCard QR code for their business card, Kaywa has some significant drawbacks:

  • No more instant free codes — Kaywa suspended their free service “due to increased misuse” and now requires verification before you can create any QR codes. What used to be a quick task now involves a signup and approval process.
  • Expensive plans — Paid plans start at $13.75/month (billed annually). The Advanced plan is $37.50/month and Premium+ is $150/month. For a handful of vCard codes, these prices are hard to justify.
  • Contact codes can’t be edited — Kaywa explicitly warns that “Contact QR Codes cannot be changed afterwards.” Their vCard codes are static, which means you’re paying a subscription for functionality that’s inherently free.
  • Server-side processing — Dynamic codes and tracking features process data on Kaywa’s servers. For privacy-conscious users, uploading personal contact information to a third-party is a concern.
  • Complexity warning — Kaywa warns that “more information equals more QR Code pixels” and recommends using “only the absolute minimum of information necessary.” A purpose-built vCard tool handles this automatically with optimized encoding.

Kaywa is a reputable platform with enterprise clients. But if your primary need is creating QR codes for business cards, a free purpose-built tool is the better fit.
