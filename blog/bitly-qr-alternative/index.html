What Is Bitly QR?

Bitly is primarily a URL shortening and link management platform founded in 2008. QR code generation is a secondary feature added to their “Connections Platform” alongside short links and landing pages. Bitly also owns QR Code Generator PRO (qr-code-generator.com), making it one of the largest players in the QR code space.

Bitly QR codes are dynamic — they link to Bitly short URLs that redirect to your destination. This means every scan goes through Bitly’s servers. The platform offers customization options including colors, gradients, dot patterns, frames, and logos (with advanced options reserved for paid plans). Scan analytics provide data on location, time, device, and browser. However, Bitly imposes strict monthly creation limits on every plan: just 2 QR codes on Free, 5 on Core ($10/mo), 10 on Growth ($29/mo), and 200 on Premium ($199/mo). For users who need a vCard QR code for a business card, Bitly’s URL-only approach and monthly limits make it a poor fit.

Why Look for a Bitly QR Alternative?

  • No native vCard support — Bitly only creates URL-type QR codes. It cannot encode contact data directly into a QR code. To share contact info, you’d need to host a digital business card page somewhere and link to it — adding complexity, cost, and server dependency.
  • Interstitial ads on free codes — In 2025, Bitly started showing interstitial ad pages before redirecting free QR code scans. Users report their codes now look “spammy” and “unprofessional” when scanned. This affects codes that were already printed and distributed.
  • Strict monthly limits — Even on paid plans, Bitly caps how many QR codes you can create each month. The free plan allows only 2. If you need codes for a team or event, you’ll hit the ceiling fast.
  • Server dependency — Every Bitly QR code scan routes through their servers. If Bitly goes down, changes their policies, or you cancel your plan, your printed business cards become useless.
  • Poor user reviews — Bitly has a 1.6-star rating on Trustpilot (114 reviews), with widespread complaints about interstitial ads appearing on existing codes, broken QR code redirects, automatic annual billing, and refusal to issue refunds.
  • Signup required — You must create an account to generate even a single QR code. For privacy-conscious users, this means handing over your email before you can start.
