Test Thoroughly for Reliable Scanning

Test your QR code business contact tips on multiple smartphones and scanning apps before printing or sharing. Maintain a white quiet zone around the code and use high contrast between foreground and background colors to ensure quick and accurate scans.

Optimize Size and Placement

For printed materials, use at least 2 x 2 cm (0.8 x 0.8 in) to guarantee reliable scanning. For digital placements like websites or email signatures, keep your code at a minimum of 100 x 100 px. Place codes prominently—in corners of business cards, footers of emails, or sidebars of web pages—for maximum visibility.

Design for Accessibility and Brand Consistency

Use brand colors thoughtfully—ensure foreground and background contrast meets WCAG guidelines. Incorporate your logo within the safe zone but limit its size to under 30% of the code area to maintain scan reliability. Provide plain versions without logos for low-light or low-resolution scenarios.

  • Maintain at least 4:1 contrast ratio for accessibility
  • Keep logos and decorative elements outside data modules
  • Offer both colored and monochrome versions
  • Test designs under varied lighting conditions

Embed and Distribute Across Channels

Embed your QR code in email signatures, digital brochures, social media profiles, and your website's contact page. For printed distributions—business cards, flyers, and signage—export at 300 DPI minimum and avoid stretching the image. Maintain and Update Your QR Codes

If your contact details change, regenerate and replace the QR code wherever it’s used to ensure consistency. Keep a record of locations where your code appears—email signatures, printed materials, and digital assets—for efficient updates. Regularly review scan analytics to identify and refresh underperforming placements.
