Why People Look for QRFY Alternatives

QRFY (qrfy.com) is a Spanish QR code platform that supports over 25 QR code types—from URLs and PDFs to menus, coupons, and vCards. It has a 4.6/5 rating on G2 and strong customization tools. For marketing teams that need dynamic codes with scan analytics, it’s a capable option.

But for users who just need a vCard QR code for their business card, QRFY has some serious drawbacks:

  • The “free trial” trap — QRFY offers a 7-day free trial, but dynamic QR codes created during the trial stop working when it expires. Multiple Trustpilot reviewers describe this as “bait and switch”—they created codes, printed them on business cards or ads, and then discovered the codes were deactivated unless they paid.
  • Expensive subscriptions — Plans start at ~$20/month billed annually. Quarterly billing is significantly higher. There’s no monthly option, and several users report being charged $120–$287 for automatic renewals without clear warning.
  • Your data lives on their servers — QRFY explicitly states: “All the QR codes that are generated with QRFY host the information on our servers.” For contact information like your phone number, email, and address, this is a privacy concern.
  • Cancellation complaints — Trustpilot reviews mention difficulties canceling subscriptions and getting refunds. One user wrote: “They will continue billing you and refuse refunds. I was forced to initiate a formal dispute with my credit card company.”
  • Static codes are an afterthought — QRFY’s business model is built around dynamic codes and subscriptions. They offer static QR codes, but the platform steers users toward dynamic codes that require ongoing payment—even when a static code would work perfectly.

QRFY is a legitimate platform with real enterprise features. But if your primary need is creating QR codes for business cards, paying a monthly subscription for something that should be free doesn’t make sense.
