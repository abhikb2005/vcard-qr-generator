vCard QR Code Format Explained

The vCard format (also called VCF) is an open standard for storing contact information. When you create a vCard QR code, the contact data is encoded inside the QR code using this structured text format. Understanding how the format works helps you create better, more compatible QR codes.

vCard 3.0 Format Structure

Most vCard QR codes use vCard version 3.0, which is supported by virtually every smartphone. Here’s what the format looks like inside a QR code:

BEGIN:VCARD
VERSION:3.0
N:Smith;Jane;;;
FN:Jane Smith
ORG:Acme Corp
TITLE:Marketing Director
TEL;TYPE=WORK,VOICE:+1-555-123-4567
TEL;TYPE=CELL:+1-555-987-6543
EMAIL:jane.smith@acme.com
URL:https://acme.com
ADR;TYPE=WORK:;;123 Main St;New York;NY;10001;USA
END:VCARD

Key vCard Fields

Field Purpose Example
N Structured name (Last;First) N:Smith;Jane;;;
FN Full display name FN:Jane Smith
ORG Company / organization ORG:Acme Corp
TITLE Job title TITLE:Marketing Director
TEL Phone number (with type) TEL;TYPE=CELL:+1-555-987-6543
EMAIL Email address EMAIL:jane@acme.com
URL Website URL:https://acme.com
ADR Address (street;city;state;zip;country) ADR;TYPE=WORK:;;123 Main St;NY;...

vCard 3.0 vs 4.0: Which Should You Use?

vCard 4.0 adds support for features like social media profiles and richer address formatting, but vCard 3.0 is the safer choice for QR codes. Here’s why:

  • Universal compatibility — vCard 3.0 works on every iPhone, Android phone, and desktop email client. vCard 4.0 has inconsistent support, especially on older Android devices.
  • Smaller data size — QR codes have a data limit. vCard 3.0’s simpler syntax keeps codes compact and scannable. More data means a denser, harder-to-scan QR code.
  • Reliable field mapping — Phone contacts apps reliably parse 3.0 fields (N, FN, TEL, EMAIL, ORG). Some 4.0-specific fields get ignored or misinterpreted.

Tip: Keep your vCard data under 300 characters for the best scanning reliability. Include only essential fields — name, phone, email, company, and website. Every extra field makes the QR code denser and harder to scan at small print sizes.

vCard QR Codes on iPhone vs Android

vCard QR codes work on both platforms, but the scanning and saving experience differs. Knowing these differences helps you create codes that work reliably for everyone.

iPhone (iOS 11+)

  • Built-in scanning — Open the Camera app and point at the QR code. A notification banner appears at the top with the contact name.
  • One-tap save — Tap the banner to open the contact card, then tap “Create New Contact” or “Add to Existing Contact.”
  • vCard 3.0 & 4.0 support — iOS handles both versions well. All standard fields (name, phone, email, address, company, title, URL) are parsed correctly.
  • Photo support — iOS can display an embedded PHOTO field, but this significantly increases QR code density and is not recommended for printed codes.

Android (varies by manufacturer)

  • Google Lens / Camera — Most Android phones (Pixel, Samsung, OnePlus) scan QR codes through the camera app or Google Lens. Tap the detected link to open the contact.
  • Inconsistent vCard 4.0 support — Some older Android devices or third-party contacts apps may not parse vCard 4.0 fields correctly. Stick with vCard 3.0 for maximum compatibility.
  • Samsung-specific — Samsung devices use Bixby Vision or the built-in camera scanner. vCard 3.0 works reliably across all Samsung Galaxy models.
  • Fallback option — If the built-in camera doesn’t detect the QR code, free apps like Google Lens or QR Code Reader work on any Android device.

vCard vs MeCard: Quick Comparison

When creating a contact QR code, you’ll encounter two formats: vCard and MeCard. Both encode contact data, but they differ in important ways.

Feature vCard (VCF) MeCard
Origin Open standard (RFC 6350) Created by NTT DoCoMo (Japan)
Data size Larger (more verbose syntax) Smaller (compact syntax)
Fields supported Name, phone, email, company, title, address, URL, photo, notes Name, phone, email, address, URL, notes
Job title & company Yes (ORG + TITLE fields) No
iPhone support Full native support Partial (may open as URL)
Android support Full native support Full native support
Best for Business cards, professional networking Simple personal contact sharing

Which Should You Choose?

Use vCard if you need job title, company name, or multiple phone numbers on your QR code — which covers most professional and business use cases. vCard is the universal standard and works reliably on both iPhone and Android.

Use MeCard only if QR code density is your top priority (e.g., very small print sizes) and you don’t need company or title fields. MeCard’s compact syntax produces a simpler, easier-to-scan code, but sacrifices important business fields.

FAQ

What's the difference between a QR code and vCard QR code?

A regular QR code can contain various types of data, while a vCard QR code specifically contains structured contact information in vCard format that phones recognize and can save directly to contacts.

Do I need a special app to scan vCard QR codes?

No, most modern smartphones can scan vCard QR codes using their built-in camera app. The phone will automatically recognize the vCard format and prompt to save the contact.

How much information can a vCard QR code store?

A vCard QR code can store name, multiple phone numbers, email addresses, company information, website URLs, and physical addresses. However, too much information can make the QR code complex and harder to scan.

What if my vCard QR code won't scan?

Make sure the QR code is printed clearly and not distorted. Use sufficient contrast and test on several devices before sharing widely.

Can I update my contact info after making a vCard QR code?

No, static vCard QR codes can't be updated once printed. For updatable info, consider dynamic QR solutions, but most business card use cases are static.
