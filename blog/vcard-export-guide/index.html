vCard Export Guide: How to Export, Import & Share VCF Contact Files

Master the art of exporting, importing, and sharing your vCard data across all devices and platforms.

Whether you're networking at conferences, updating your digital business card, or migrating contacts to a new device, understanding how to export and import vCard files is essential. The vCard format (stored as .vcf files) serves as the universal language for contact information, enabling seamless sharing between iPhones, Android devices, email clients, and CRM systems. This comprehensive guide walks you through every aspect of working with vCard exports—from generating QR codes with your contact data to troubleshooting import issues across different platforms. By the end, you'll be equipped to manage your digital contact information like a professional.

Understanding vCard Exports

When you export contact information, you're creating a VCF (Virtual Contact File) that contains structured data in a standardized format. The vCard specification, now in version 4.0, defines how contact details are organized and stored. A typical vCard export includes fields for full name, phone numbers (mobile, work, home), email addresses, physical addresses, company name, job title, website URLs, and even profile photos.

The beauty of the VCF format lies in its universality. Unlike proprietary formats that lock you into specific ecosystems, VCF files work across all major platforms. The file itself is plain text, making it easy to view and edit with any text editor if needed. Each field follows a specific syntax—for example, TEL;TYPE=CELL:+1234567890 indicates a mobile phone number. This standardization ensures that when someone imports your vCard, all your information appears correctly in their contacts app.

Exporting from Phone Contacts

Already have contacts on your phone that you want to convert into QR codes? Both iOS and Android make it straightforward to export existing contacts as VCF files.

On iPhone: Open the Contacts app, select the contact you want to export, scroll down and tap "Share Contact." Choose how you want to share it—you can AirDrop, email, or save the VCF file to your Files app. From there, you can upload it to our generator or use the data to create a QR code.

On Android: Open your Contacts app, tap the three-dot menu, and select "Export." You can export individual contacts or all contacts at once. The resulting VCF file saves to your Downloads folder. For Google Contacts, you can also export via contacts.google.com by selecting contacts and choosing "Export" from the menu.

Once exported, you can open the VCF file to review its contents, then use that information to generate a professional vCard QR code that others can scan.

Importing vCard Files

When someone scans your vCard QR code or receives your VCF file, they need to import it into their contacts. The good news is that modern devices handle this automatically. Upon scanning a vCard QR code, smartphones display the contact information and offer an "Add to Contacts" button—one tap and they're done.

For VCF files received via email or messaging apps, the process is equally simple. On iOS, tapping the attachment opens it in Contacts with an option to create a new contact or add to an existing one. Android devices behave similarly, prompting users to choose which account to save the contact to (Google, Samsung, etc.).

Desktop users can import VCF files directly into their email clients. In Outlook, go to File → Open & Export → Import/Export, then select "Import a vCard file." Gmail users can visit contacts.google.com, click "Import" in the sidebar, and upload the VCF file. Learn more about the VCF contact file format for detailed technical information.

Cross-Platform Compatibility

One of vCard's greatest strengths is its universal compatibility. Here's how different platforms handle vCard imports:

  • iOS (iPhone/iPad): Native support in the Camera app for QR scanning and the Contacts app for VCF files. Works seamlessly with iCloud sync.
  • Android: Built-in QR scanning in Google Camera and most third-party camera apps. Contacts app handles VCF imports directly.
  • Microsoft Outlook: Full VCF import support on both desktop and web versions. Contacts sync across Microsoft 365 devices.
  • Gmail/Google Contacts: Web interface supports VCF import. Syncs automatically to Android devices signed into the same Google account.
  • macOS Contacts: Double-clicking a VCF file opens it directly in Contacts. Supports drag-and-drop import as well.

For the best compatibility, use vCard version 3.0 (which our generator uses by default). While version 4.0 offers additional features, version 3.0 ensures the widest device support. Read our complete vCard QR code guide for more details on format specifications.

Troubleshooting Export/Import Issues

Occasionally, vCard exports or imports don't work as expected. Here are common issues and their solutions:

  • QR code won't scan: Ensure adequate lighting and hold your phone steady. If the code is printed too small (under 2cm), it may be difficult to read. Try increasing the size or reducing the amount of data encoded.
  • Missing fields after import: Some apps don't support all vCard fields. Custom fields or notes may not transfer. Stick to standard fields (name, phone, email, address) for best results.
  • Character encoding issues: Special characters or non-Latin scripts sometimes display incorrectly. Ensure your VCF file uses UTF-8 encoding. Our generator handles this automatically.
  • Duplicate contacts created: If importing creates duplicates, use your contacts app's "merge duplicates" feature. Most platforms offer this in their settings.
  • VCF file won't open: The file may be corrupted. Try re-exporting from the source, or open in a text editor to check for obvious formatting errors.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a VCF file?

A VCF (Virtual Contact File) is the standard file format for storing contact information. It contains details like name, phone numbers, email addresses, and more in a structured text format that all major platforms can read.

Can I export multiple contacts into one vCard file?

Yes, most contact apps allow you to select multiple contacts and export them as a single VCF file. Each contact is stored as a separate vCard entry within the file.

Will my vCard QR code work on all phones?

Yes, vCard QR codes work on virtually all modern smartphones. Both iOS and Android devices can scan QR codes with their built-in camera apps and recognize vCard data automatically.

How do I update my contact info after creating a QR code?

Static vCard QR codes cannot be updated after creation. You'll need to generate a new QR code with updated information. For updatable codes, consider dynamic QR solutions that redirect to editable landing pages.

Why won't my VCF file import correctly?

Common issues include special characters in fields, unsupported vCard versions, or corrupted files. Try opening the VCF in a text editor to check for encoding issues, or re-export with a different app.

Can I include a photo in my vCard export?

Yes, the vCard format supports embedded photos. However, adding images significantly increases the QR code complexity, which may affect scannability. For QR codes, keep images small or omit them.
