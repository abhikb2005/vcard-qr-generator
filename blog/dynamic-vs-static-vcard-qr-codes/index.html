What is a Static QR Code?

A static QR code encodes data directly into the QR pattern itself. Once created, the information cannot be changed—it's permanently "baked in."

How it works:

  1. You enter your contact info (name, phone, email, etc.)
  2. The generator encodes this directly into the QR pattern
  3. When scanned, the phone reads data from the QR code itself
  • No internet required: Works offline—data is in the code
  • Free forever: No subscription or hosting fees
  • Cannot be changed: Need to update info? Generate a new QR code

What is a Dynamic QR Code?

A dynamic QR code doesn't store your data directly. Instead, it contains a short URL that redirects to your actual content. You can change where the URL points without changing the QR code.

How it works:

  1. The QR code encodes a short URL (e.g., qr.example.com/abc123)
  2. When scanned, the phone visits that URL
  3. The URL redirects to your vCard download or landing page
  4. You can change the destination anytime via a dashboard
  • Editable: Update your contact info without reprinting
  • Trackable: See scan counts, locations, devices
  • Requires internet: Won't work if the server is down
  • Usually paid: Most services charge monthly fees

When to Use Each Type

✅ Use Static QR Codes When:

  • Your contact info rarely changes
  • You want a free, permanent solution
  • Privacy matters (no scan tracking)
  • You need offline functionality
  • You're printing on permanent materials (tattoos, plaques, gravestones)

✅ Use Dynamic QR Codes When:

  • You change jobs or phone numbers often
  • You're running a marketing campaign and need analytics
  • You've printed 10,000 flyers and might need to update the URL
  • You want A/B testing or retargeting capabilities
  • You're a business with compliance/audit requirements

vCard QR Codes: Static or Dynamic?

For personal networking and business cards, static vCard QR codes are usually the better choice. Here's why:

The Reality Check:

  • Most people don't change their contact info that often
  • If you do change jobs, you'll likely want new business cards anyway
  • Static QR codes save your contact directly to the phone—no redirect, no delay
  • Dynamic QR services can shut down, making your codes useless

However, dynamic vCard QR codes make sense if you're a company issuing cards to employees who might leave, or if you're a real estate agent who changes listings frequently.

The Verdict

For most individuals: Use static vCard QR codes. They're free, work forever, don't depend on any service, and your contact info probably won't change that often anyway.

For businesses & marketers: Consider dynamic QR codes if you need analytics, A/B testing, or the ability to update URLs at scale.

Our recommendation: Start with a free static vCard QR code. If you later need analytics or editability, you can always create a dynamic one for specific campaigns.
