How to Customize Your vCard QR Codes

Brand your QR codes with colors, logos, and layouts that match your identity.

A custom QR code is a tiny billboard. Make yours recognizable without sacrificing the reliability that scanners depend on every day.

Choose the right color palette

Contrast is the hero of every scannable QR code. Start with a light background and a darker foreground shade that meets accessibility guidelines.

If you incorporate gradient backgrounds, keep them subtle. Excessive variation can confuse older camera sensors.

  • Stick to brand colors that meet WCAG contrast ratios
  • Test gradients by printing a small sample
  • Avoid pure white foreground elements—they reduce legibility

Integrate your logo

Center logos within the safe zone of the QR code. Maintain a generous margin so scanners can still detect the finder patterns.

Use a transparent PNG or SVG version of your logo to keep edges crisp on both digital and printed surfaces.

  • Keep the logo under 30% of the total QR area
  • Avoid placing text labels directly on top of modules
  • Offer a plain version for low-light scanning scenarios

Select supporting materials

Match the QR code style to the medium: glossy cards, matte stickers, or on-screen overlays all require slight tweaks.

Consider adding microcopy below the code that clarifies the benefit of scanning—help your audience understand the value immediately.

  • Use vector formats for large-format printing
  • Laminate frequently handled cards to avoid fading
  • Refresh signage each quarter to keep branding aligned
