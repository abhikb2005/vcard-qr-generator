Design Considerations for Business Card QR Codes

Size and Dimensions

For business cards, size your QR code between 2cm and 3cm (0.8-1.2 inches) square. This provides reliable scanning while leaving room for your name, logo, and other card elements. Never go smaller than 2cm—tiny QR codes fail to scan reliably.

Color and Contrast

  • High contrast is essential — Dark modules on a light background scan best
  • Black on white works best — Other color combinations work if contrast is maintained
  • Avoid gradients — Solid colors ensure consistent scanning
  • Match your brand — Dark blue, green, or burgundy on white can complement your card design

Quiet Zone

Always maintain a white border (quiet zone) around your QR code—at least 4 modules wide. This margin helps smartphone cameras identify where the code begins and ends. Crowding other design elements too close causes scanning failures.

Adding Your Logo

Want to brand your QR code? Our logo QR code generator lets you place your company logo in the center. Keep logos small (15-20% of the total QR area) so error correction can compensate for the covered modules.

Where to Place the QR Code on Your Card

Strategic placement affects both aesthetics and scannability. For detailed printing specifications, see our QR code printing guide.

Back of Card (Recommended)

Placing the QR code on the back keeps your front design clean and professional. You can use a larger code (up to 3cm) since there's more space, and include a brief call-to-action like "Scan to save contact." This approach works well for traditional and minimalist card designs.

Front of Card

If you prefer front placement, position the QR code in a corner—typically bottom right. Keep it at 2cm to leave room for essential text elements. This placement works best for tech-forward brands where the QR code reinforces a modern image.

Printing Best Practices

  • Export your QR code at 300 DPI or higher for sharp printing
  • Use matte or semi-gloss finishes—high gloss can cause glare that interferes with scanning
  • Avoid textured or embossed areas over the QR code
  • Test the printed card with multiple phones before ordering large quantities

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a business card QR code generator?

A business card QR code generator creates scannable codes containing your contact information. When printed on your card and scanned, it saves your details directly to the recipient's phone contacts.

What size QR code fits on a business card?

The ideal size is 2-3cm (0.8-1.2 inches). This balances reliable scanning with leaving room for other card elements. Never go smaller than 2cm for consistent results.

Should I put the QR code on the front or back?

The back is more popular since it keeps the front clean for branding. However, front placement in a corner works well for tech-forward designs. Either location works if you maintain proper size and quiet zone.

Can I add my logo to the QR code?

Yes! Our Pro generator lets you add a custom logo to the center of your QR code. Keep it small (15-20% of the code area) to maintain scannability.

What information should my business card QR code contain?

Include name, phone, email, company, and job title at minimum. You can add your website and LinkedIn profile, but avoid overloading—too much data creates dense codes that are harder to scan.

Do business card QR codes expire?

No. Static vCard QR codes never expire because the data is embedded in the code pattern itself. They work indefinitely without internet or subscriptions. Create a new code if your contact info changes.
