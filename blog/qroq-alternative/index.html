What Is Qroq?

Qroq (getqroq.in) is a SaaS QR code platform built by Teknikforce, aimed at businesses and marketers who need branded, dynamic QR codes. The platform supports 23+ QR code types and focuses on visually designed codes with custom colors, logos, and dot styles. Qroq’s core value proposition is dynamic QR codes that let you change the destination URL after printing, combined with scan analytics and hosted landing pages.

Beyond basic QR generation, Qroq offers a point-and-click page builder for creating QR-powered landing pages — menus, business profiles, link-tree style pages, lead capture forms, and social media link pages — all hosted on their servers without requiring your own domain or hosting. It also includes an AI-powered page creator, URL shortening, and CSV lead export. The Elite Monthly plan costs $47/month and includes 100 dynamic QR codes with a 30,000 dynamic scan cap per month. The yearly plan is $297. There is no free tier. For someone who simply needs a vCard QR code on a business card, Qroq is significant overkill.

Why Look for a Qroq Alternative?

  • No native vCard encoding — Qroq offers “v-card templates” as hosted landing pages, but the QR code points to a server-hosted page, not a vCard 3.0 file encoded directly in the QR pattern. When someone scans it, they see a webpage — not an instant contact-save prompt on their phone.
  • Paid only — $47/month — There is no free tier. The cheapest plan is $47/month ($297/year), and it includes a 30,000 dynamic scan limit. For a simple contact QR code, that’s a significant ongoing cost with zero benefit over a free static vCard code.
  • Server dependency — All dynamic QR codes and hosted pages route through Qroq’s servers. If your subscription lapses or their service goes down, your QR codes stop working — a critical problem for printed business cards.
  • Scan limits — The Elite plan caps dynamic scans at 30,000 per month. If you’re using QR codes on widely distributed materials, you could hit this ceiling and lose functionality mid-campaign.
  • Privacy concerns — Qroq’s analytics track every scan — location, device, time — which means contact data and scan behavior route through their servers. For privacy-conscious users, this is a dealbreaker.
