HOOK: QR codes are readable even when they are damaged. That is not luck.

BODY:
One reason QR codes work so well is error correction.

Depending on the QR code settings, part of the code can be dirty, scratched, blocked, or damaged and still scan correctly.

That is why logos can sometimes sit inside QR codes.

That is why printed codes can survive imperfect surfaces.

That is why a phone does not need a perfect lab environment to read them.

But this does not mean every design choice is safe.

A code that works on your monitor might fail on glossy paper, tiny print sizes, poor lighting, curved packaging, or low contrast designs.

The QR code may be robust.

Your design choices may not be.

There is a difference.

Marketing tends to blur it.

CTA: Test your QR code on real paper before printing in volume.
