QR codes were not born in marketing.

They were born in car factories.

The original problem was not "how do we make menus touchless?"

It was tracking automotive parts more efficiently.

That matters because QR codes were built for speed, reliability, and machine readability.

Marketing adopted them later because the format happened to solve a broader problem:

How do you move information from a physical surface into a digital action quickly?

That is why QR stands for Quick Response.

Very literal naming. Refreshing, honestly.

The marketing world sometimes treats QR codes like a gimmick. But the underlying technology came from operations, logistics, and manufacturing.

Which is probably why it works best when the use case is practical.

Look at one printed asset today and ask whether it has a real next step.
